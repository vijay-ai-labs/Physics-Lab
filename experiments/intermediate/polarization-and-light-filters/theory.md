# Polarization & Light Filters

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** Light is an electromagnetic wave that oscillates. Ordinary light (like from the sun or a bulb) is unpolarized, meaning it vibrates in all possible directions perpendicular to its travel. A polarizer is a filter that specifically blocks all light waves except those vibrating in one specific direction (its transmission axis).
**Real-World Example:** Polarized sunglasses! Sunlight scattering off a shiny road or body of water becomes partially polarized horizontally (causing intense annoying glare). Polarized sunglasses are made with a vertical transmission axis specifically to block these horizontal waves, eliminating the glare.

## Formulas Used
**Malus's Law** dictates how much light passes through a second perfect polarizer (an analyzer) given an already polarized beam of light hitting it.

$$
I = I_0 \cos^2(\theta)
$$

Where:
*   $I$ is the Final Intensity of the light that successfully passes through.
*   $I_0$ is the Initial Intensity of the light striking the polarizer.
*   $\theta$ is the angle between the light's initial polarization direction and the axis of the new polarizer filter.

If the two filters are crossed at exactly 90° ($\theta = 90^\circ$), then $\cos(90^\circ) = 0$, meaning absolutely no light passes through!

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How do polarizing filters block light waves?** to understand how adjusting the angle of linear filters dramatically alters the intensity of transmitted electromagnetic waves according to Malus's Law.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Initial Amplitude ($I_0$):** The brightness/intensity of the original light source before hitting any filters.
*   **Filter 1 Angle (°):** Rotates the first polarizing plate. Since the incoming light is unpolarized, this filter will always trap exactly 50% of the light, but it defines the new polarization angle for the beam.
*   **Filter 2 Angle (°):** Rotates the second analyzing plate. Changing the relative difference between Filter 1 and Filter 2 demonstrates Malus's Law. Setting them 90° apart achieves a total blackout.
*   **Wave Visualization:**
    *   Notice how the rotating 3D light wave flattens from a chaotic multi-directional beam into a perfect 2D ribbon after the first filter!
