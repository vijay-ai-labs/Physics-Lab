# Wave Interference

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** When two waves travel through the same medium and occupy the same space, they superimpose (add together) to form a new wave. This is called interference. 
**Real-World Example:** Noise-canceling headphones use *destructive interference*. They listen to outside noise and emit a sound wave perfectly inverted (opposite). When the two sound waves meet at your ear, they cancel each other out, leaving silence!

## Formulas Used
The Principle of Superposition states that the resultant displacement ($y_{\text{net}}$) at any point is the algebraic sum of the displacements of the individual waves ($y_1$ and $y_2$):

$$
y_{\text{net}}(x,t) = y_1(x,t) + y_2(x,t)
$$

Assuming two sine waves:
1.  **Constructive Interference:** When the peaks of wave 1 line up perfectly with the peaks of wave 2 (in phase). They add together to create a massive wave. ($y_{\text{net}} = 2 \cdot y_{\text{max}}$)
2.  **Destructive Interference:** When the peak of wave 1 lines up with the trough of wave 2 (out of phase). They cancel each other out, resulting in zero amplitude. ($y_{\text{net}} = 0$)

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How do two waves combine when they meet?** to visually understand the mathematics of superposition and how waves can build each other up or destroy each other.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Amplitude: Wave 1 & 2:** Controls the height of the respective source waves.
*   **Frequency: Wave 1 & 2:** Controls how fast the wave cycles (number of waves per second). Finding common multiples creates stable beats.
*   **Phase Wave 2 (°):** Shifts the second wave forward or backward. An exact 180° shift causes perfect destructive interference if amplitudes and frequencies match!
*   **Visual Overlays:**
    *   **Wave 1 (Blue) / Wave 2 (Green):** The original source waves traveling in isolation.
    *   **Result (Yellow):** The live mathematical sum ($y_1 + y_2$) that you would actually hear or see in reality.
