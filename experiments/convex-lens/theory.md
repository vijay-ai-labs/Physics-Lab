# Convex Lens (Ray Optics)

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** A convex lens (or converging lens) is thicker at the center than at the edges. Because of refraction, light rays traveling parallel to the principal axis bend inward as they pass through the glass, converging and intersecting perfectly at the focal point.
**Real-World Example:** Magnifying glasses, reading glasses, microscopes, and the lens inside your very own eye are all convex lenses. They are used to bend spreading light paths back together to form focused images. If you use a magnifying glass to burn a leaf with sunshine, you are using the lens to converge parallel sun rays directly onto the leaf at the focal point!

## Formulas Used
The **Thin Lens Equation** predicts where an image will form when looking through a lens:

$$
\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_o} + \frac{1}{d_i}
$$

Where:
*   $f$ is the focal length of the newly shaped lens.
*   $d_o$ is the object distance (distance from object to lens).
*   $d_i$ is the image distance (distance from image to lens). 

**Magnification ($M$):**
$$
M = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = -\frac{d_i}{d_o}
$$
*   If $M$ is negative, the image is inverted (upside down). This happens when $d_o > f$.
*   If $M$ is positive, the image is upright and virtual (acting as a magnifying glass). This happens when $d_o < f$.

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How do convex lenses bend light to form images?** to visually understand how reversing object distances can flip an image upside down or greatly magnify it.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Object Distance ($d_o$):** Move the green object closer or further. Try placing it exactly between the Focal Point ($f$) and the Lens!
*   **Object Height ($h_o$):** Make the source object taller or shorter.
*   **Focal Length ($f$):** Changing this simulates modifying the curvature of the glass lens. Thicker, curvier glass has a much shorter focal length.
*   **Ray Tracing Lines:**
    *   **Parallel Ray:** Travels straight from the object tip, hits the lens, and refracts precisely through the right focal point.
    *   **Center Ray:** Passes straight through the dead center origin of the lens perfectly unbent.
