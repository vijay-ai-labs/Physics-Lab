# Archimedes' Principle

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** Archimedes' Principle states that the upward buoyant force that is exerted on a body immersed in a fluid, whether fully or partially submerged, is equal to the weight of the fluid that the body displaces.
**Real-World Example:** When you get into a full bathtub, the water level rises because your body pushes the water out of the way (displacement). In return, that displaced water pushes back up against you, which is why your body feels much lighter underwater! If you placed a boat in the tub, it would displace an amount of water weighing exactly as much as the boat itself.

## Formulas Used
The Buoyant Force ($F_B$) is equal to the weight of the displaced fluid. Since weight is equal to mass ($m$) times gravity ($g$), we can write:

$$
F_B = m_{\text{fluid}} \cdot g
$$

Because fluid mass is hard to measure directly, we calculate it using the fluid's density ($\rho$) and the submerged volume of the object ($V_{\text{sub}}$):
$$
m_{\text{fluid}} = \rho_{\text{fluid}} \cdot V_{\text{sub}}
$$

Therefore, the main formula for Buoyant Force is:
$$
F_B = \rho_{\text{fluid}} \cdot V_{\text{sub}} \cdot g
$$

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How does the weight of displaced fluid relate to buoyancy?** to mathematically prove why heavy objects like steel cargo ships can float, while small heavy objects like coins sink immediately.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Mass (kg):** Adjusts the weight of the yellow block. A heavier block is pulled down harder by gravity ($F_g = mg$).
*   **Volume (L):** Adjusts the physical size of the block. A larger block displaces more water, dramatically increasing the upward buoyant force.
*   **Fluid Density (kg/m³):** The density of the liquid pool. Water is 1000 kg/m³. If you change this to 1300 kg/m³ (like the Dead Sea), the upward push is much stronger!
*   **Visual Overlays:**
    *   **Gravity:** The red arrow pulling straight down into the pool.
    *   **Buoyancy:** The blue arrow pushing straight up. Notice when Buoyancy exceeds Gravity, the block bobs to the surface!
