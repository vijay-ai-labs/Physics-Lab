# Gravity Simulator

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** Gravity is the fundamental force of attraction that exists between any two masses, any two bodies, any two particles. It is the force that pulls objects toward each other.
**Real-World Example:** When you drop an apple, it falls to the ground because the Earth's gravity pulls it downward. Similarly, gravity is what keeps the Moon in orbit around the Earth and the Earth in orbit around the Sun.

## Formulas Used
Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation states that every particle attracts every other particle in the universe with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

Where:
*   $F$ is the gravitational force between the masses.
*   $G$ is the gravitational constant ($6.674 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$).
*   $m_1$ and $m_2$ are the masses of the two objects.
*   $r$ is the distance between the centers of the two masses.

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **What is the gravitational force between two masses?** to help us understand how changing the mass of objects or the distance between them directly impacts the strength of the gravitational pull.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Mass 1 (kg):** Adjusts the mass of the first object ($m_1$). Increasing this will proportionally increase the gravitational force.
*   **Mass 2 (kg):** Adjusts the mass of the second object ($m_2$). 
*   **Distance (m):** Adjusts the distance ($r$) between the centers of the two objects. Notice how decreasing the distance significantly increases the force due to the inverse-square law.
*   **Vector Overlays:**
    *   **Force:** Toggles the visual representation of the gravitational force vectors pulling the masses toward each other.
