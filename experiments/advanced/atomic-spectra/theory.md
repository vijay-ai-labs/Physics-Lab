# Atomic Spectra (Bohr Model)

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** The Bohr Model of the atom proposes that electrons orbit the nucleus in strictly defined, quantized, circular "shells" or energy levels. An electron can instantly jump from one level to another by absorbing or emitting a photon with energy perfectly equal to the difference between the two shells.
**Real-World Example:** Neon signs! When high voltage is passed through a glass tube filled with neon gas, the electricity excites the neon electrons up to higher orbits. When they naturally fall back down, they emit photons of specific red-orange wavelengths, creating that iconic vibrant glow. Different gases (like Argon or Helium) have differently spaced energy levels, resulting in entirely different glowing colors.

## Formulas Used
The energy assigned to the $n$-th orbit of a Hydrogen-like atom is calculated as:

$$
E_n = -\frac{13.6 \text{ eV}}{n^2}
$$

Where:
*   $n$ is the principal quantum number (the shell number: 1, 2, 3...)
*   $-13.6\text{ eV}$ is the ground-state energy. Negative because the electron is bound to the nucleus.

When an electron transitions from a higher energy initial state ($n_i$) to a lower energy final state ($n_f$), it releases its surplus energy as a single photon:
$$
\Delta E = E_{n_i} - E_{n_f} = 13.6 \text{ eV} \cdot \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)
$$

The wavelength ($\lambda$) of that emitted photon is then found via Planck's equation:
$$
\lambda = \frac{hc}{\Delta E} \approx \frac{1240 \text{ eV}\cdot\text{nm}}{\Delta E}
$$

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How do electrons jumping between orbits emit specific light colors?** to visually grasp quantum jumps and predict the exact spectral line generated by a specific orbital drop.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Initial State ($n_i$):** The high-energy outer orbit where the electron begins.
*   **Final State ($n_f$):** The lower-energy inner orbit where the electron acts to fall to.
*   **Trigger Jump:** Click the button to force the transition. Watch the electron instantly teleport and emit the colored photon sine wave!
*   **Visual Overlays:**
    *   **Energy Level Graph:** Dynamically maps how much energy (eV) is lost during the gap.
    *   **Spectrometer:** Captures the emitted photon and maps it on a rainbow spectrum so you can see if the transition created visible light, UV, or Infrared!
