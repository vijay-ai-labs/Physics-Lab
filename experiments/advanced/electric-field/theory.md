# Electric Field

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** An electric field is an invisible region surrounding a charged particle where a force would be exerted on other charged particles. It is the mechanism by which charges push or pull on each other at a distance without physically touching.
**Real-World Example:** When you rub a balloon on your hair to build up static electricity, your hair stands up on end reaching for the balloon. Your hair is physically trapped inside the invisible, attractive electric field generated by the charged balloon surface!

## Formulas Used
The magnitude of the Electric Field ($E$) created by a single point charge ($Q$) at a specific distance ($r$) is given by Coulomb's Law:

$$
E = k \cdot \frac{|Q|}{r^2}
$$

Where:
*   $E$ is the Electric Field strength (Newtons per Coulomb, N/C).
*   $k$ is Coulomb's constant ($8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$).
*   $Q$ is the magnitude of the source charge.
*   $r$ is the radial distance away from the charge.

When there are multiple charges (like a positive and negative pair), the *net* electric field at any point in space is simply the vector sum of the individual fields from all present charges (Superposition Principle). Field lines always point *away* from positive charges and *towards* negative charges.

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How do charged particles manipulate the electric field vectors around them?** to visually grasp vector superposition and the inverse-square falloff of electromagnetic forces.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Charge 1 (Positive):** Adjusts the strength of the red source charge. A higher charge creates a stronger repelling field pushing outwards.
*   **Charge 2 (Negative):** Adjusts the strength of the blue source charge. A higher charge creates a stronger attractive sink pulling inwards.
*   **Distance:** Adjusts the physical separation between the two charges. Moving them far apart makes their fields act in isolation. Moving them very close creates intense, highly curved dipole field lines bridging the gap.
*   **Vector Field Overlay:**
    *   The background is populated by tiny compass arrows that track the local Electric Field vectors. Their brightness/opacity indicates the strength of the field at that exact coordinate, while their rotation maps the direction a tiny positive test particle would be pushed!
