# Magnetic Field of a Wire

## What Is This Experiment?
**Definition:** Electromagnetism dictates that whenever an electrical current flows through a conductive wire, it generates its own circular magnetic field wrapping around the length of the wire. 
**Real-World Example:** This is the foundational principle for nearly all electric motors, generators, and speakers. Inside a speaker, an electromagnet is placed next to a permanent magnet. As alternating current flows through the wire coil, its magnetic field rapidly flips, causing it to attract and repel against the permanent magnet. This frantic vibration creates the sound waves you hear!

## Formulas Used
The Right-Hand Rule is a physical mnemonic: If you point your right thumb in the direction of the electrical current ($I$), your curling fingers will dictate the circular rotational direction of the magnetic field ($B$).

Mathematically, the magnitude of the Magnetic Field ($B$) at a specific distance ($r$) away from a long, straight wire is calculated using Ampère's Law:

$$
B = \frac{\mu_0 \cdot I}{2 \pi \cdot r}
$$

Where:
*   $B$ is the Magnetic Field strength (measured in Teslas, T).
*   $I$ is the Current flowing through the wire (Amperes, A).
*   $r$ is the radial distance away from the center of the wire (meters, m).
*   $\mu_0$ is the Permeability of Free Space (a magnetic constant, $4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$).

This formula proves two things: turning up the current linearly increases field strength, and stepping away from the wire severely drops the field strength.

## Goal of the Experiment
The main objective is to find **How does electrical current generate wrapping magnetic fields?** to visualize the invisible, circular magnetic loops swirling around power lines according to Ampère's Law and the Right-Hand Rule.

## Simulation Controls
*   **Current ($I$):** Adjust the amperage flowing through the central vertical wire. A high positive current (flowing Upwards) creates a strong counter-clockwise magnetic spin.
*   **Reversing Flow:** Try setting the current to a negative value. Notice how the visual field vectors instantly snap to rotating clockwise, perfectly matching your right thumb down!
*   **Vector Field Overlay:**
    *   Hundreds of tiny compass needles map the 2D plane around the wire. They dynamically re-orient themselves to trace the perfect invisible circular tracks of the electromagnetic force.
